About
My name is Matt Agius and I am a programmer from Cary, North Carolina. I am a 19 year old student, currently attending UNC Charlotte. I have a passion for computer programming, and I plan to achieve a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science.
Some of my high school achievements include Certification in 6 Adobe Programs and 3 Microsoft Certifications. These certifications demonstrate fluent knowledge and understanding of the software being tested. I was accepted into the National Technical Honor Society (NTHS) in my junior year. NTHS is a commitment and accepts only those who "cultivate excellence in today’s highly competitive, skilled workforce." I am also a member of the Black Falcons Cyber Security Team at Green Hope High School. My team members and I compete in National Cyber Security Competitions against teams from all over the country. We even used a flying video drone to live stream our football team's Homecoming game (USA Today Article)(Triangle Business Journal Article) to generate donations for registration fees. On my own, I have also had videos games, posters, and videos I created over the years featured by teachers and shown to students.
I have created work in a variety of digital mediums such as digital graphics and design, programming video games and interactive media, and video production and filming. Using Adobe Photoshop and other Adobe programs I have made a wide array of work ranging from posters to web animations for school projects and club's needs. If I could create something that may be useful, I was always happy to help out. In programming classes I regularly created games and software in VB and C#. Before winter break, we had a computer set up in the hall where students could play games made in class by students. Everyone was very proud with how well the event turned out. After having these experiences I was inspired to produce a short documentary about The Cargo Hold, the programming classroom I was taught in. I filmed the classroom for months and collected hours of footage to use in the film. It was a wonderful learning experience that required a lot of time and commitment.
While at Green Hope High School, I also ran Cross Country for all 4 years. I kept running in Winter and Spring Track most years, only missing one season. I am a captain for all of these sports where I use leadership skills to lead many runners through the workouts. Outside of school, I have experience volunteering with the Y.M.C.A. with their Y-Guides and Princesses program. I have been volunteering for the past 5 years at their various camps in North Carolina. As a trained volunteer I have been helping with camp activities and other needs. As well, I am working a part-time job as a cashier at The Hot Dog Stand at The Raleigh Flea Market. I started the job in the summer of 2013 and have continued working through the school year. My references for these activities are available upon request.
